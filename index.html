Prayani Trace

Batch-level traceability from the Hart, MI kitchen to the shelf built as a working concept for the Blockchain Developer application.

Scan a jar. See its journey.

Every batch of Prayani raita is logged at each handoff production, distribution, and shelf into a tamper-evident chain. A customer (or a store, or Prayani itself during a recall) can look up any batch ID and see exactly where it's been, with cryptographic proof the record hasn't been altered.

Jar QR codes

In production, this QR would be printed on the lid. Scanning it opens this same look-up, pre-filled with that jar's batch ID.

Log a chain-of-custody event

This simulates what a kitchen lead, a Lipari warehouse scan, or a Kroger receiving check-in would do one entry per handoff. Each entry is hashed together with the one before it, so editing old history breaks the chain visibly.

Add a stage to an existing batch, or start a new one.

What this could grow into

The traceability system above is the foundation it's what's actually built and working. These two ideas extend the same batch/event data to other parts of the business, but they're deliberately left as roadmap, not built, since they only make sense once the core system is proven and each depends on a partner (Lipari, or a rewards platform) buying in.

Future · Customer-facing

Loyalty rewards on scan

Each time a customer scans a jar's QR code to verify it, they'd earn a small reward token redeemable for a discount turning the trust feature into a repeat-purchase driver. Reuses the same batch/scan data already being logged, with no new backend.

Future · Partner-facing

Distributor handoff automation

Right now, a Lipari or Kroger receiving scan would be logged manually into this system. A further step would let that scan trigger the next step automatically e.g. confirming delivery, which is the kind of thing that could eventually tie into payment or inventory reconciliation. This needs Lipari's systems to participate, so it's a partnership conversation before it's an engineering one.

Built as a scoped concept demo hash-chained in the browser to illustrate the mechanism, not deployed to a live blockchain network.
